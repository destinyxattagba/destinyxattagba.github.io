General Information

Côte d'Ivoire -- also known as Ivory Coast -- is a West African country situated in between Ghana, Guinea, Liberia, and Mali. It is one of the most prosperous West African countries due to various export products and foreign investments. The country's name comes from its involvement in the ivory trade along the coastline. Côte d'Ivoire boasts tropical weather that spans three seasons: warm and dry, hot and dry, and warm and wet. Its flag is orange, green, and white, the reverse of the Irish flag.

Brief History

Previously colonized by France, Côte d'Ivoire gained independence on August 7, 1960, led by its first president, Felix Houphouet-Boigny. Despite some political instability, the country has largely maintained peace and close ties with neighboring nations and France. The legacy of French colonization is still visible, particularly in the French language and the use of the African Franc as currency.

Economic Development

Côte d'Ivoire is a fast-growing economy, largely based on agriculture. It is the world’s top cocoa bean producer, along with coffee, palm oil, and rubber. The country also engages in mining and oil production, and its GDP per capita was about $7,000 in 2023.

Daily Life

Urban areas like Abidjan showcase a mix of tradition and modernity, with markets, restaurants, and nightlife influenced by both Western culture and Ivorian traditions. In rural areas, agriculture remains the primary activity.

Government and Politics

Côte d'Ivoire is a presidential republic, with Alassane Ouattara as the president since 2010. The political system is bicameral, with the Senate and National Assembly forming the legislature. Yamoussoukro is the official capital, while Abidjan serves as the administrative hub.

Education

The education system in Côte d'Ivoire includes preschool, primary, secondary, and higher education, with schooling mandatory until age 16. Challenges include overcrowded classrooms and disparities in resources, but the government is making efforts to improve literacy and education quality. Prominent institutions include:

  • Université Félix Houphouët-Boigny in Abidjan
  • Université Nangui Abrogoua
  • Université Alassane Ouattara
